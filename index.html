field notes

A field journal for working with places and things. Keep notes across as many site visits as your research takes. These entries invite you to record what you see and experience, and then respond to those happenings through speculation, drawing out new relations, and asking some more questions about what might come next. Stamp each entry with the date, time, weather, and place, write as you observe, and your notes are saved right here to return to, add to, and export whenever you need them.

“Sometimes the aim of research may be to disrupt the reassurance of the apparently familiar (where, you suspect, the very familiarity can be obscuring) precisely by rendering it strange.”
Doreen Massey, “Imagining the Field” In Using Social Theory: Thinking through Research, ed. Michael Pryke, Gillian Rose, and Sarah Whatmore (London: Sage, 2003), 80.
